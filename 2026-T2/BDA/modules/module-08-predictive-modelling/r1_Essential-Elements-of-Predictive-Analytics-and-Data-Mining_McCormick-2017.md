- This is the definition of data mining that I have used and refined over many years. Data mining is the selection and analysis of data, accumulated during the normal course of doing business, to find and confirm previously unknown relationships that can produce positive and verifiable outcomes through the deployment of predictive models when applied to new data. There's a couple of points I want to emphasize. The data's not new. We weren't creating this data for the purpose of doing some kind of an experiment. The data was collected for business reasons. We're not testing hypotheses. These are previously unknown relationships. Yet, we need proof. We need verifiable outcomes. It's got to be useful, it has to serve a purpose within our business, and it's got to work on new data. In short, to benefit, we have to deploy it.
