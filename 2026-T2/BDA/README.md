# Big Data & Analytics - BDA601

## Subject Introduction
This subject teaches students techniques and best practices in collecting, storing, extracting, cleaning, manipulating, visualising and analysing useful information from large structured, semi-structured, or unstructured data sets, which are critical for organisational decisions. Students will also learn applications of big data analytics across industry sectors such as segmentation and prediction, recommender systems and targeted marketing, sentiment analysis, operational analytics, and Big Data for social good.

## Subject Details
- Subject length: 12 weeks, or intensive over 6 weeks
- Credit points: 10
- Workload: 10 hours per week for 12-week delivery, or 20 hours per week for 6-week delivery
- Prerequisite: CCF501 Cloud Computing Fundamentals
- Specialist equipment: computer/laptop and relevant specialist software
- Prescribed texts: none

## Subject Learning Outcomes (SLO)
- `a)` Explain and evaluate the V's of Big Data: Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity, Valence, and Value.
- `b)` Identify best practices in data collection and storage, including data security and privacy principles.
- `c)` Apply data science principles to cleaning, manipulation, and visualisation of data.
- `d)` Design analytical models based on given problems.
- `e)` Effectively report and communicate findings to an appropriate audience.
