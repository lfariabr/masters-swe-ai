# Deep Learning - DLE602

## Subject Introduction
Deep learning is a subset of machine learning, and machine learning is a subset of Artificial Intelligence (AI). This subject introduces the basics of deep learning and how to build neural networks. Students are presented with different methods and applications across AI sub-areas such as natural language processing, speech recognition, and computer vision. The subject begins with simple neural networks such as multi-layer perceptrons and progresses to more complex concepts such as recurrent neural networks, convolutional neural networks, and long short-term memory networks.

## Subject Details
- Subject length: 12 weeks, or intensive over 6 weeks
- Credit points: 10
- Workload: 10 hours per week for 12-week delivery, or 20 hours per week for 6-week delivery
- Prerequisite: MFA501 Mathematical Foundations of AI
- Specialist equipment: computer/laptop and relevant specialist software
- Prescribed texts: none

## Subject Learning Outcomes (SLO)
- `a)` Build, train, and apply deep learning models to real-world tasks.
- `b)` Compare and select ways to pre-process signals, images, and texts for natural language, speech recognition, and computer vision applications.
- `c)` Develop critical analysis skills in deep learning research.
- `d)` Demonstrate collaborative skills to apply deep learning to solve real-world problems.
- `e)` Demonstrate ability to effectively communicate scientific and technical information.
