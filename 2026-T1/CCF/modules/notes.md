### Introduction
During the past two decades, the IT and telecom sectors have taken a huge leap in terms of development. New devices have evolved - like tablets, Internet of Things-based (IoT) wearable technologies, AR/VR devices, smart phones, and more. Access to the internet has been simplified, made easy and economical. Simultaneously, the applications and user demands have made underlying algorithms complex. New experiences like virtual reality and the trend to include artificial intelligence in applications have increased the need for higher computational power. The popularity of wearables and linked devices like the Google Home Mini or Alexa has meant that IoT is generating more data. All of this is then feeding AI systems essential to support these IoT devicesâ€™  learning capabilities. Applications of the Internet have evolved from search and email to videos, financial applications, video games, etc. Platforms are now not only computers but mobile phones, tablets and AR lenses.

The computing model has also changed to keep up with the demands and expectations of consumers. Rather than being distributed in nature, a more centralised approach â€“ in the form of cloud computing is becoming increasingly popular as this requires less computational and storage capabilities on user terminals and more on the centralised system (cloud). This in turn makes user terminals less expensive and simple and at the same time makes it cost effective for developers and businesses to deploy the solutions and offer services efficiently. In addition, business-driven big data necessitate a centralised forest of supercomputers â€“ that is a cloud computing system. Cloud computing aims to provide cost effective resource-rich computing solutions to businesses.

This subject provides a fundamental understanding of cloud computing. You will be able to understand how the cloud computing infrastructure has evolved from the traditional IT infrastructure and what business advantages it brings (including its total cost of ownership). In addition, you will also learn the different cloud segments and cloud deployment models and the key players in the market. This subject also provides knowledge of cloud services and cloud security.
