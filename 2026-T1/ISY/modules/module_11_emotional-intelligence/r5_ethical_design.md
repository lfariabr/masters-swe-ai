# Ethical Design for Intelligent Systems

## About this Course

As a result of the recent convergence of a transformative set of AI and intelligent systems, technologies are entering a new era. Intelligent systems refer to advanced machines that focus on interactions with humans in dynamic physical and social environments. Such machines may range from pet robots, such as the Artificial Intelligence Robot (AIBO), which was first introduced in 1999, to Apple’s smart assistant, Siri, which is one of the most popular personal assistants today. 

However, the exponential growth of AI and advances in intelligent systems has also started a discourse about their ethical use in which the following questions have been raised: 
‘Will AI replace human workers?’, 
‘Will AI increase the rise of fake media and disinformation?’ and 
‘Are the decision-making processes of intelligent systems transparent?’.

In this course, you will be introduced to the ethical design of intelligent systems. The course begins by providing you with an overview of the concepts critical to intelligent systems and ethics. This course comprises four components:

1. A conceptual introduction to AI, intelligent systems, ethics and the need for ethics in intelligent systems;
2. An elaboration on how ethics should be considered in the design of intelligent systems. Specifically, this component of the course considers:
    - the ethical issues that arise in relation to intelligent systems;
    - the design principles of intelligent systems; and
    - the relationship between ethics and design.
3. An examination of fairness and the bias issues commonly found in intelligent systems. In this component:
    - Bias refers to a prejudice in favour of (or against) one thing, person or group (as compared to another) in a manner that is usually considered unfair; and
    - The approaches that can be adopted to mitigate bias and discrimination in intelligent systems are also explored.
4. A consideration of explainable AI in intelligent systems—an emerging topic in technologies.

## Course Objectives

- Describe the ethical issues and design principles in intelligent systems;
- Analyse bias in intelligent systems; and
- Apply explainable AI models in intelligent systems.
