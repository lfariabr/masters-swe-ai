Release Document for a website to be launched in several Asian countries
Social media + networking website (linkedin) w/o non-english support. As a result, website needs a focus release document to be created highlithing sveral areas of functionality that have to be supported

Thailand, 5 areas of focus
•	Cultural tendencies in this region – what area areas of importance / focus for this culture?
o	Hierarchy / Respect for seniority → communication reflects status 
o	High social media engagement → visual, informal communication styles
o	Indirect communication → gentle language expected 
o	Collectivist orientation → group belonging & harmony matter 
o	Kreng Jai → avoiding confrontation, being considerate 
o	Nicknames widely used (important for identity fields) 
•	Social expectations of the internet in this region: what does this culture typically use the internet for? Is it widespread? Does connectivity need to be increased?
o	Preferred platforms: LINE, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube
o	Messaging platforms are central to daily life → LINE integration expected
o	Internet penetration is high, but rural areas may have slower speeds → lightweight mobile UX required
o	Short-form video is huge (TikTok culture)
o	Thailand is one of the highest social media usage countries in the world 
o	Internet usage is mobile-first (95% on smartphones)
•	Any other regional issues that may prove problematic?
o	Digital inequality between urban x rural regions
o	Uneven internet quality outside Bangkok/Chiang Mai
o	High mobile usage but lower laptop usage
o	Strong reliance on LINE, meaning new apps must integrate well 
o	Concerns on data privacy (PDPA law)

1. Introduction: Purpose of the Thai market release and cultural localization requirements.
2. Cultural & Social Behavior Insights (Thailand): Key Thai cultural tendencies that directly impact feature design (hierarchy, politeness, collectivism, social harmony, mobile-first culture).
3. Feature Focus Areas: Five localization priorities (language support, UI cultural adaptation, privacy norms, mobile optimization, content filtering, etc.)
4. Social Harmony Features (High Power Distance + Politeness Norms): UX adaptations for indirect communication, conflict-avoidance, respectful phrasing.
5. Local Ecosystem Integration (LINE + Thai Job Platforms): Critical integrations for adoption in Thailand’s digital ecosystem.
6. Infrastructure & Regulatory Considerations: PDPA (data privacy), internet variability, censorship rules, government digital policies
7. Accessibility & Inclusivity Requirements: Support for older users, rural users, mobile-only users, economic accessibility.
8. Risks & Mitigation Plan: Potential cultural, technical, or adoption risks—and how the website mitigates them.
9. Conclusion: Why these adaptations ensure a successful Thai launch.
